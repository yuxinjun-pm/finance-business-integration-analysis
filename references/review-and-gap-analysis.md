# Review and Gap Analysis

Use this reference when the user gives you a final PPT, XLSX, model, or polished answer and asks you to compare it against your earlier answer, identify the gap, summarize lessons, or raise your future standard.

## Review objective

Do not only say:
- I was right
- I missed some numbers

Instead identify:
- where your direction was correct
- where the final delivery is methodologically stronger
- what exact capability was missing
- how to turn that into a reusable improvement

## Comparison dimensions

Use these dimensions:

### 1. Metric rigor
Check:
- Did you define the profit lens clearly?
- Did the final submission define the analysis boundary better?
- Did you confuse contribution profit with accounting net profit?

### 2. Calculation depth
Check:
- Did you compute by object, or just reason qualitatively?
- Did the final submission quantify totals, counts, erosion, or segment economics?

### 3. Segmentation depth
Check:
- Did you stop at binary judgment?
- Did the final submission create management-relevant segments?

### 4. Driver specificity
Check:
- Did you identify specific drivers or just broad labels?
- Did the final submission rank or quantify driver differences?

### 5. Recommendation quality
Check:
- Were your recommendations generic?
- Did the final submission quantify pricing, event reduction, thresholds, or service boundaries?

### 6. Implementation quality
Check:
- Did you provide a mechanism or only a conclusion?
- Did the final submission define monthly tracking, model use, or review cadence?

### 7. Validation quality
Check:
- Did the final submission validate a model, classification, or rule set?
- Did you ignore that layer entirely?

## Gap categories

Classify missed points into one or more of:
- missing metric definition
- missing object-level calculation
- missing total picture
- weak segmentation
- vague driver analysis
- unquantified recommendations
- no improve-vs-exit boundary
- no implementation mechanism
- no validation/model layer

## Output structure

Recommended response structure:

1. Overall judgment
2. Where my earlier answer was directionally right
3. Where the final delivery is materially stronger
4. Root cause of the gap
5. Reusable lessons
6. Future SOP or checklist

## Important rule

The final goal is not self-criticism.

The final goal is to build a repeatable upgrade path so that future answers naturally reach the stronger standard.
